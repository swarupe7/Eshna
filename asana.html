Yogaasanas for menustral cycle for Women

Yoga can be a beneficial practice for managing menstrual symptoms and promoting overall menstrual health. While there are various yoga poses (asanas) that can be helpful, it's important to listen to your body and choose poses that suit your comfort level and needs. Here are some yoga asanas that are commonly recommended for menstrual cycle support:

1. Child's Pose (Balasana):

This pose can help relieve lower back pain and provide gentle stretching for the abdomen. Kneel on the floor, sit back on your heels, and stretch your arms forward while resting your forehead on the ground.

2. Cat-Cow Pose (Marjaryasana-Bitilasana):

This dynamic movement helps release tension in the lower back and abdomen. Start on your hands and knees, arch your back while inhaling (Cow Pose), and round your back while exhaling (Cat Pose).

3. Supine Twist (Supta Matsyendrasana):

This twist can alleviate back pain and improve circulation to the pelvic area. Lie on your back, bend your knees, and drop them to one side while keeping your shoulders on the ground. Repeat on the other side.

4. Bridge Pose (Setu Bandhasana):

Bridge Pose can help relieve discomfort in the lower back and pelvic region. Lie on your back, bend your knees, and lift your hips off the ground, keeping your feet and shoulders on the mat.

5. Legs-Up-the-Wall Pose (Viparita Karani):

This restorative pose can reduce bloating and ease menstrual cramps. Sit with your hips against the wall, then swing your legs up the wall while lying on your back.

6. Seated Forward Bend (Paschimottanasana):

This pose stretches the spine, hamstrings, and lower back, promoting relaxation and alleviating discomfort. Sit with your legs straight and fold forward from the hips.

7. Reclining Bound Angle Pose (Supta Baddha Konasana):

This pose opens the hips and can help with menstrual cramps. Lie on your back with the soles of your feet together and knees dropped to the sides.

8. Corpse Pose (Savasana):

End your practice with Savasana to relax the body and mind. Lie on your back with arms and legs extended, and focus on deep, calming breaths.

9. Gentle twists:

Gentle spinal twists, like Half Lord of the Fishes (Ardha Matsyendrasana), can help relieve tension in the back and promote circulation in the pelvic area.

10. Pranayama (breathing exercises):

Deep, mindful breathing can reduce stress and discomfort. Try slow, deep breaths (diaphragmatic breathing) or the Nadi Shodhana (alternate nostril breathing) technique.


Always practice yoga with awareness of your body's limitations and any discomfort. If you experience severe pain or discomfort during your menstrual cycle, it's best to modify or skip poses as needed. Yoga should be a gentle and supportive practice during this time. If you have any underlying medical conditions or concerns, consult with a healthcare provider or a certified yoga instructor for personalized guidance on yoga for your menstrual health.
